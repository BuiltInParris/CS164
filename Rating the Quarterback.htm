A Nod to the QB Rating*

We know 100 is good, 50 is bad, and 158.3 is the absolute best possible. How to get there is a mystery we hoped to unravel.

Here is the actual formula:

C=completions, Y=yards, T=touchdowns, I=interceptions, A=attempts

Lou Bloomfield, a University of Virginia physics professor who teaches a course called How Things Work, explains the concept:

As long as a passer hasn’t saturated one of the measures by doing so well or so poorly that he gets a special-case score of zero or 2.375, the calculation simplifies enormously. A little basic algebra reduces the whole thing to:


Completion percentage x 0.833
+
yards x 4.167
+
touchdown percentage x 3.334
+
interception percentage x 4.166
+
2.083
